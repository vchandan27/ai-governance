# Acme Corp Responsible AI Policy

## 1. Purpose and scope
This policy governs the development and deployment of artificial intelligence
systems across Acme Corp. It applies to all employees, contractors and
third-party vendors that build or operate AI on behalf of the company.

## 2. Governance and accountability
The AI Governance Board, chaired by the Chief AI Officer, owns this policy.
Roles, responsibilities and authorities for AI risk are assigned across the
product, engineering, legal and compliance teams. The board reviews this policy
at least annually and maintains a documented quality management approach.

## 3. Risk management
We maintain an iterative risk assessment process for every AI system. Each
system is reviewed for potential harms to health, safety and the fundamental
rights of individuals before launch, and identified risks are mitigated and
tracked as residual risk.

## 4. Data governance
Training, validation and testing data are reviewed for quality and
representativeness. We document the provenance of datasets and examine them for
bias before use, and we record any gaps or deficiencies.

## 5. Human oversight
All customer-facing automated decisions include a human-in-the-loop. Trained
operators can interpret outputs and can override or stop the system at any time.

## 6. Transparency
We inform natural persons when they are interacting with an AI system and
provide clear instructions for use to internal deployers.

## 7. Monitoring and incidents
Deployed systems are monitored in production. Incidents and errors are logged,
reviewed by the governance board, and feed into continuous improvement.
